Chemical change is irreversible, meaning that chemical change is difficult to reverse.
Chemical change also creates new products (e.g. water)

Examples: Burning paper, Roasting a marshmallow

Physical change is reversible, meaning that physical change is easy to reverse.
Physical change does NOT create new products.

Examples: Phase changes, Deforming shape

The subscript tells us how many atoms of that type is in the molecule. Sodium oxide has 2 Na(Sodium) atoms.
If there is no subscript, it is assumed to be 1.

The coefficient tells us how many molecules of that type will be reacted/produced.
In this case, the reaction produces 2 sodium oxide molecules.
